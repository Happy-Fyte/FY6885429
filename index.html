Abstract

Augmented reality (AR) is an emerging technology in the digital media space where images, videos, sounds, or 3D models can be overlayed onto the real world in real time through smart devices. AR has been successfully implemented in games like Pokémon GO and in online retail, where consumers can interact with 3D models of products, enhancing their shopping experience.

Introduction

Online shopping lacks the tactile experience of in-person shopping. Augmented Reality bridges this gap by providing consumers with a more interactive and realistic product experience, reflecting the product's quality more effectively than traditional e-commerce methods.

Analysis and Discussion

Many examples of AR have been very successful like through games like Pokémon GO where 3D models of the Pokémon would be rendered into the real world to allow for a seamless experience leading to it becoming an instant hit. A recent development in AR was through the consumerism base through implementing products for sale in 3D models so that people can interact with the product they are buying without physically being at the store to pick it up and use it. This lets shoppers virtually explore items in their own environments, enhancing the online shopping experience by making it more immersive and informative even without visiting a store in person. In the beauty industry specifically this has been a major development as beauty products are a main focus in many studies as AR has been the most effective in this area. Cognitive control, the ability to evaluate options effectively, plays a crucial role in AR experiences. Studies show that positive peer opinions can enhance a consumer's understanding and trust in a product. AR helps consumers better predict product performance, leading to higher purchase intention. However, gender effects were not considered in the study as it focused solely on lipstick products.

Figure showing Likert Scale scores comparing AR Experience vs Non-AR Experience

The data tells a compelling story. Consumers using AR reported a significantly higher purchase intention (5.039 on a 6-point Likert scale) compared to those in non-AR environments (2.88). This stark difference underscores AR's ability to foster trust and confidence in product evaluation. With features like virtual try-ons or 3D product visualizations, AR eliminates uncertainty, allowing shoppers to feel more secure about their choices. AR isn’t just about showing off products; it’s about building trust, helping people make better decisions, and ultimately boosting sales. This isn’t just a theory. A recent study found that shoppers using AR had 75% higher purchase intention compared to those who didn’t Aditya et al. (2023). Why? Because AR takes the guesswork out of shopping. Instead of imagining how a product might look, shoppers can see it for themselves whether it’s makeup on their face or a couch in their living room. This hands-on approach makes them feel confident about their choices. But it’s not just about wanting to buy. AR also helps shoppers feel in control. The study revealed that AR users scored much higher on behavioural control Aditya et al. (2023) so basically, their confidence in making smart decisions. This reflects what we’re seeing across the industry: today’s shoppers don’t just want to buy something; they want to feel good about their decision. AR makes that possible by providing instant, interactive feedback. For retailers, AR isn’t just about keeping up with trends, it’s about meeting real consumer expectations. Shoppers today want tools that are helpful, intuitive, and reliable.
