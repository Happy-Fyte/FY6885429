Abstract

Augmented reality (AR) is an emerging technology in the digital media space where images, videos, sounds, or 3D models can be overlayed onto the real world in real time through smart devices. AR has been successfully implemented in games like Pok√©mon GO and in online retail, where consumers can interact with 3D models of products, enhancing their shopping experience.

Introduction

Online shopping lacks the tactile experience of in-person shopping. Augmented Reality bridges this gap by providing consumers with a more interactive and realistic product experience, reflecting the product's quality more effectively than traditional e-commerce methods.

Analysis and Discussion

Cognitive control plays a crucial role in AR experiences. Studies show that positive peer opinions can enhance a consumer's understanding and trust in a product. AR helps consumers better predict product performance, leading to higher purchase intention. However, gender effects were not considered in the study as it focused solely on lipstick products.
